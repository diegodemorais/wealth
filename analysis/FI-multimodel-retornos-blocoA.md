## 2. Developed Market Ex-US Premium vs. US Equity

### Historical Evidence
- **DMS (1900–2023)**: US equities outperformed developed market ex-US by **~1.0–1.5% annually** in real USD terms over the 20th century. However, this gap narrowed significantly in the 2000s.
- **Fama-French data**: The "size and value" premiums exist within DM ex-US, but the *country* return premium is not robustly documented as a persistent alpha source post-costs.

### Post-2010 Reality
- **2010–2023**: US equities dramatically outperformed DM ex-US (e.g., ~13% vs. ~6% annualized real returns), driven by:
  - Superior earnings growth and profit margin expansion in US tech giants.
  - US exceptionalism in productivity and innovation.
  - Favorable demographic and fiscal policies.

### Arguments For/Against DM Ex-US Outperformance (Next Decade)

**For DM ex-US**:
1. **Valuation discount**: DM ex-US trades at ~30% lower CAPE than US (as of 2024), providing a "margin of safety."
2. **Mean reversion**: Historical periods of US outperformance (e.g., 1990s) were followed by DM ex-US leadership.
3. **Sector composition**: DM ex-US has more exposure to financials, industrials, and energy—potentially beneficiaries of reshoring and energy transition.

**Against DM ex-US**:
1. **Earnings growth gap**: US companies have demonstrated superior earnings growth, particularly in high-margin software/tech.
2. **Innovation concentration**: US tech giants continue to dominate AI and cloud computing.
3. **Home bias**: Global investors already have significant US exposure; DM ex-US may remain underloved.

**Consensus**: Most forward-looking models (e.g., Research Affiliates) project **modest underperformance** of DM ex-US relative to US over the next decade (~1–2% annually), but with high uncertainty.

## 4. Most Underestimated Risk for Brazilian Investors Holding International Equity in USD/GBP

### The Overlooked Risk: **Currency (FX) Risk on Returns**

While Brazilian investors often focus on equity risk, the **real return in BRL** can be dramatically affected by currency movements:

1. **Historical impact**: From 2010–2023, the BRL depreciated from ~1.7 to ~5.0 per USD—a ~65% loss in BRL terms for a USD-denominated portfolio, even if the USD portfolio broke even in real terms.
2. **Forward risk**: Brazil's fiscal dynamics and high real interest rates create ongoing BRL volatility. A 30–50% BRL depreciation over a decade would erase much of the equity return premium.
3. **Hedging costs**: Long-dated FX hedges are expensive and imperfect for a 50-year horizon.

### Why This Is Underestimated
- Most return projections are presented in **USD**, not BRL.
- Investors may assume "USD is safe," but the **conversion back to BRL** is the relevant return.
- Academic research (e.g., Bekaert & Hodrick) shows currency risk is a significant component of international portfolio volatility, often exceeding equity risk over short periods.

### Mitigation
- Consider a **gradual BRL-hedged equity allocation** (though costly and imperfect).
- Maintain some **BRL-denominated assets** (e.g., Brazilian real Treasury bonds) to hedge liabilities.
- Recognize that **currency diversification** is limited when the base currency is a high-volatility emerging market currency.
